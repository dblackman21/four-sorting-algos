Music Production Basics

Core Components

Digital Audio Workstation (DAW): the software used to record, edit, and arrange audio and MIDI.

Audio Interface: converts analog signals (microphones, instruments) into digital audio for your DAW.

Monitors / Headphones: accurate playback for mixing and critical listening.

MIDI Controller: sends note and control data to software instruments and plugins.

Stages of a Track

Recording: capturing audio and MIDI performances.

Editing: cleaning takes, comping, quantizing, and arranging clips.

Mixing: balancing levels, panning, EQ, compression, and effects to create a cohesive stereo mix.

Mastering: final polishing for loudness, tonal balance, and distribution readiness.

Typical Signal Chain

Microphone -> Preamp -> Audio Interface -> DAW (record) -> Plugins (EQ, Compression, Reverb) -> Mix -> Master

Tips

Start with gain staging: keep headroom and avoid clipping. Use reference tracks when mixing. Learn one plugin deeply rather than many superficially.
